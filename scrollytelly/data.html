How are Senior Australians being targeted by Scammers?

The rise in scams targeting senior Australians is a call to action. By increasing awareness, enhancing digital literacy, and implementing stronger safeguards, we can help protect our elderly from falling victim to these ruthless crimes.

Discover how scams are increasingly targeting Australians aged 65 and over.

Scam Trends Among Senior Australians
From January 2020 to June 2024, over 120,000 scams targeting senior Australians have been reported.

During this period, the frequency of reported scams has increased significantly year over year, reflecting the growing sophistication and adaptability of scammers. These malicious actors continually devise new tactics to exploit the vulnerabilities of older Australians, preying on their trust and often limited familiarity with digital technologies.

The escalating trend in scam reports underscores the urgent need for heightened awareness, education, and protective measures to safeguard our senior population from these evolving threats.
Rising Scam Incidents
Over the past few years, scammers have been increasingly preying on the elderly, with reports of scams involving Australians aged 65 and over surging. This growing trend highlights the need for greater awareness and education among seniors to protect themselves from these malicious activities.
Dominance of Phishing Scams
Among the various types of scams, phishing stands out as the most prevalent threat to senior Australians. These scams, which cunningly mimic legitimate communications to steal personal information, have caused significant distress and financial losses among the elderly.
Email and Mobile as the Primary Contact Mode
In an era where everyone has an email address and mobile, scammers have increasingly turned to emails, phone calls and text messages as their weapons of choice. For seniors, a simple email or text can lead to devastating financial losses, as these messages often appear harmless but are designed to deceive.
Geographical Concentration in Certain States
While no part of Australia is immune to scams, certain states like New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland have emerged as hotspots for fraudulent activities targeting seniors. This geographical disparity is likely linked to specific states having higher populations and commercial activity.
Gender Differences in Scam Victimisation
When it comes to targeted scams, gender plays a role in the experience of senior Australians. Men, in particular, have been found to be targeted more significantly in certain types of scams, highlighting a need for awareness campaigns that address the specific risks faced by each gender.
High Financial Impact of Scams
For many seniors, the financial impact of falling victim to a scam can be devastating, wiping out savings and causing long-term financial insecurity. The sheer magnitude of the losses reported highlights the ruthless nature of these scams and the urgent need for protective measures. In over 300 reported cases, senior Australians have lost over $1 million.

The Role of Awareness and Reporting
As awareness about scams grows, so too does the number of reported incidents. The data likely represents just the tip of the iceberg, as many seniors may not report scams due to shame, embarrassment, or simply not realizing they've been scammed.
