Think You've Been Scammed? Here's What to Do.

  • Stay calm and act fast. If you suspect you've been scammed, it's important to act quickly to minimize damage.
  • Stop any communicationStop any communication with the scammer.
  • Change your passwords for any online accounts that might be affected, especially banking or financial accounts.
  • Contact your bank or financial institution to report the scam and freeze or monitor your accounts.
  • Report the scam to the appropriate authorities (e.g., Scamwatch, your local consumer protection agency, police, or SeniorSafe)
  • Consider contacting a cybersecurity expert or your financial institution for further support.
  • Lastly, visit our Phishing Scam Resources page to learn about different types of phishing scams so you can identify and avoid them in the future.
