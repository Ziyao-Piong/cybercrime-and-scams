Spear Phishing

Spear phishing is like a scam message made just for you.

Imagine a scammer pretending to be someone you know, like a bank employee or a friend. They send you an email or a text that looks very real, maybe saying something like, "There's a problem with your account" or "Click here to update your information." Because the scammer knows some personal details about you, the message seems trustworthy, almost as if it came from someone you know.

But in reality, they’re trying to trick you into clicking a link or giving them important information like your password or bank card number. This type of scam is especially dangerous because it's “designed just for you,” making it much harder to spot than a mass-sent scam.

Real-Life Examples

Between 2013 and 2015, Evaldas Rimasauskas impersonated a Taiwanese hardware supplier, Quanta Computer, whose services were used by two tech giants, Google and Facebook. He sent out fake invoices worth $122 million ($99 million and $23 million to Google and Facebook, respectively) to these companies for almost three years until he was arrested and prisoned for 30 years.

How to Prevent

  1. If someone asks for personal info suddenly, verify their identity by calling or contacting them through another way.
  2. Look closely at the sender's email to make sure it’s really from someone you know. Scammers often use addresses that look almost the same.
  3. Before clicking a link in an email, hover your mouse over it to check if it leads to a trusted website.
  4. Turn on two-factor authentication for your accounts. Even if someone gets your password, they won’t be able to log in easily.
